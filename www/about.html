Great Food, For a Great Cause We set out with a couple very important factors in mind. Create an event that showcased the great culinary talent that Palm Beach County has to offer and raise awareness to local community causes. Everyone loves great food and there isn't anything better than enjoying great flavors while being among friends and family. Each year we have continued to grow and expand our guests' favorite experiences and this year will be no different. The 2016 Feast of the Sea will be held October 22, 2016 starting at 11am inside the Meyer Amphitheater. What is slated to be one of the most memorable 1 day seafood and culinary extravaganzas to date, this year will involve 24 of the best Palm Beach County chefs representing their amazing respective restaurants and brands. During the FREE daytime feastivities guests enjoy great food and beverages, demonstrations, live music, and much more. One new addition is the Knife fights; hourly chef competitions where all the chefs will stop everything a compete in some friendly chef battles for cash. One knife battle is the ‘Slice and Dice’ where each chef will be given ingredients to slice and dice as fast as they can inches from guests faces for their chance at winning some beer money. The night time transitions into a private grand tasting; 4 hours of heated chefs competition. For the 3rd year; 16 of Palm Beach Counties best culinary marksmen will create sparks in front of an amphitheater full of people and projected on a large LED wall. 4 rounds will determine the last chef standing and be crowned 2016 Maestro del Mar. As well as, the proud new owner of an over-sized plastic check worth 5000 dollars. This portion of the day is a private event and exclusive passes go on sale August 1st and will sell out. A portion of each pass sold goes to several of our local organizations including our main beneficiaries, Ernie Els - Els for Autism Foundation that will be starting a culinary program on campus and be open to all individuals on the spectrum. As well as, Future 6 Helping Hand Fund; this local organization provides free action sports camps - Surfing, Skating, Fishing - for kids with special needs and at-risk youth. The synergy between the 2 organizations is really something we are excited about this year. We have a great beach party planned that includes all the kids from both sides. This will take place prior to this year's Feast of the Sea.
